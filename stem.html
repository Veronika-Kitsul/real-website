INITIAL PLAN

Chaos Theory was discoverec by Lorenz in 1960s when he studied weather patterns. Lorenz noticed that starting with slightly different initial conditions totally changed how the patterns developed and one could not predict in what direction would it move. That is why synoptics are not able to predict weather with a 100% precision — it is chaotical. Another famous example of the Chaos Theory is the Butterfly Effect — butterfly's fly in Brasil can cause a rainstorm in China, though this is a bit exagerrated, it gives a good understanding of the concept of Chaos Theory. Because Chaos is so unpredictable, there were thoughts of using it in Cryptography to obtain truly random encryption of messages that are believed to be 100% unhackable.
